How the Swedish school system works

A visual guide for expat families · No Swedish required

The most important thing to know: In Sweden, school placement is based on queue time and your registered address (folkbokforing) - not exam results or applications. The earlier you join queues, the better your options.

The path from arrival to school place

Types of school in Sweden

Kommunal skola

Municipal school

Run by the municipality. Free. Your guaranteed fallback. Quality varies by area. All instruction in Swedish unless specifically bilingual.

Friskola

Independent school

Privately run but publicly funded and free to attend. Must follow the same curriculum. IES, Futuraskolan, Vittra, Montessori are friskolor.

Internationell skola

International school

Teaches IB or foreign curriculum. Often has fees. Not required to follow Swedish curriculum. Good if you may move again internationally.

Did you know?

Did you know
All schools in Sweden are free to attend - including friskolor. The government pays a voucher (skolpeng) that follows the child to whichever school they attend.
Did you know
Forskola hours are heavily subsidised. Families pay at most around 1,572 SEK/month for the first child (2024 maxtaxa rates). Often much less.
Did you know
Every Swedish school is legally required to offer free school meals (lunch). Sweden is one of very few countries in the world that does this.
Did you know
If your child speaks a language spoken by at least 5 pupils at the school, the school must offer free mother tongue (modersmal) instruction.
Note on accuracy: This guide reflects the Swedish Education Act (Skollagen) and Skolverket guidelines as of 2024-2025. Individual municipalities may have slight variations. Always verify specifics at your local kommunens website or by calling them directly.
