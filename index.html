School types
Three paths. Same cost. Very different queues.
Every school in Sweden is free. What differs is who runs it, how you get in, and how long you wait.
Kommunal skola
Municipal school
State-funded. Every child is guaranteed a place, assigned by home address. No application. Same national curriculum. Free meals and materials. Just show up.
No queue needed
Friskola
Independent free school
Privately run, publicly funded. Still completely free. Often has a theme: Montessori, language focus, sport. Must apply and join a queue. Popular ones fill years ahead.
1 to 3 year wait
International school
English or IB curriculum
English-medium teaching. IB or British curriculum. Some are free. Others charge tuition. The queues are the longest of all. Register before you book your moving van.
2 to 4 year wait

What every Swedish school shares
No uniforms. No entry tests. Free lunches. Parent involvement is welcome and normal. Schools communicate in Swedish by default. Ask for English summaries on letters home. Rain gear is not optional.
Queue system
The biggest surprise for every expat parent.
Position in the queue equals the date you registered. That is the only thing that matters. Not grades, not postcode, not income.
How it actually works
Friskolors and international schools run their own waiting lists. The municipality has no control over them. The earlier you add your child, the earlier your position. You can register from abroad before you even have a Swedish address.
Register the day you know you're moving. Not when you arrive. Not when you have your address. Today.
What if you miss the queue entirely?
You will not be left without a school. Your local kommunal skola is legally required to take your child regardless of timing. The queue only affects your friskola and international school choices.

Glossary
Every Swedish school term, translated into plain English.
You will see these words on every official site and school letter. Now you will know exactly what they mean.
School stages
Förskola
Preschool, ages 1 to 5
Subsidised childcare for working parents. Fees capped at around 1,572 SEK per month (2024 to 2025). Demand far exceeds supply in cities. Join queues immediately.
Förskoleklass
Reception year, age 6, compulsory
Mandatory bridge year before grade 1. Play-based but more structured than preschool. Your local school must give your child a place.
Grundskola
Primary and lower secondary, ages 7 to 15
Nine years of compulsory schooling. Free meals. No selection whatsoever. Stages: lågstadiet (1 to 3), mellanstadiet (4 to 6), högstadiet (7 to 9).
Gymnasium
Upper secondary, ages 16 to 19, voluntary
Equivalent to sixth form or A-levels. Students choose from national programmes. Grade-based admission. This is the one place academic results actually matter.
System terms
Friskola
Independent free school, no fees, queue required
Same public funding per pupil as municipal schools. Accepts students strictly by queue position. Can specialise in pedagogy or language.
Maxtaxa
Government fee cap for childcare
Limits what any family pays for förskola regardless of income. Around 1,572 SEK per month for first child in 2024 to 2025. Less for siblings.
Skolverket
The national school authority
Sets curriculum and quality standards. Not your contact for enrolment. Your local utbildningsförvaltning handles placement.
Utbildningsförvaltning
Local education office, your actual first contact
Handles school placement, assignment, and disputes. Search "[your city] utbildningsförvaltning" to find yours.
Personnummer
Swedish personal ID number
You can join queues without one. You need it to confirm your school place once you arrive.

Language support
Your child does not need to speak Swedish to start school.
Sweden's system has real, legal language support built in. You just need to know what to ask for.
For your child, SvA
Svenska som andraspråk
Swedish as a second language. Every public school must offer this. Ask for it on day one. Do not wait to be offered it. It runs alongside regular classes, not instead of them.
For your child, modersmål
Mother tongue tuition
Every school must arrange tuition in your child's first language if enough children in the municipality share it. Usually one session per week after school. Ask the office to set it up.
For you, SFI
Svenska för invandrare
Free Swedish classes for adults. You are legally entitled to these from day one of residence. Sign up at your local vuxenutbildning (adult education centre). Flexible around work hours.
Free. No waiting list.
Real talk
School letters arrive in Swedish
Use Google Translate. Ask the school if they can send English summaries. Most will say yes without hesitation.
Kids pick it up faster than you expect
Swedish children are famously welcoming. Language acquisition through play is remarkably quick. Parents are usually more stressed about this than the kids are.
FAQ
Questions every expat parent asks. Answered honestly.
No fluff, no government-speak. Just the real answers.
Can I choose any school in the city?
You can apply to any friskola or kommunal skola. But your nearest kommunal school is assigned by default. Choice is opt-in and depends on queue position.
Do my kids need Swedish on day one?
No. Schools are legally required to provide language support. Children typically pick up Swedish within a year through peers, sometimes faster.
Are there uniforms?
None. Ever. Swedish schools have no uniforms. Casual, weather-appropriate clothes. Excellent waterproof gear is more important than what brand it is.
What does the school day look like?
Usually 8:00 to 15:00. Lunch is free and cooked on-site. After-school care called fritids is available and subsidised for ages 6 to 12.
What if we move mid-year?
Your local school must place your child. No waiting until September. Language support starts from day one. This happens all the time and schools handle it well.
Can we visit before enrolling?
Yes. Email the rektor (headteacher) directly and ask. This is completely normal. Most will arrange a tour and a meeting within a week or two.
My child has special needs. How does this work?
Swedish schools are legally required to support every student regardless of need. Talk to the school's specialpedagog (special education coordinator) as early as possible.
How much homework will my child get?
Very little, by design. The Swedish system believes learning happens at school. This surprises most expat families. It is not a sign that standards are low.
